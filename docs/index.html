Nothing to migrate, nothing to unwind

The switch is not a one-way door. This is the part that matters most if you have years of mail you are not willing to risk.

📡

Your mail stays put

PigeonPost connects to your existing account over IMAP and keeps a local, searchable copy for offline use. For an IMAP account there is nothing to import on the way in and nothing stranded on the way out. Stop using PigeonPost and your mailbox is exactly where it always was.

🔁

Your calendar and contacts move both ways

Calendars import and export as .ics (RFC 5545). Contacts import and export as vCard or CSV. Both round-trip with Outlook and Thunderbird, so what comes in can always go back out. A .eml file opens straight in the app and on Windows PigeonPost can be your default .eml handler.

Your worries, answered

The questions a Thunderbird or Outlook user actually asks, answered plainly. Where the answer is no, we say so.

Will I have to migrate years of mail?

No. PigeonPost talks to your IMAP account and caches a local copy. The mail itself never leaves your server.

What if I try it and want to go back?

Then you close it and nothing has changed. Your accounts were never moved, so they are still live in your old client. Your calendar and contacts export back out in standard files.

Does my Gmail or iCloud account work?

Yes, through an app password. A wizard fills in the servers for Gmail, iCloud, Yahoo, Zoho, Fastmail and StartMail.

I use a Google Workspace account for work.

That one will not work here. Workspace is OAuth-only, so an app password is refused by Google. Personal Gmail is fine with an app password.

Is my Microsoft password safe?

You sign in through Microsoft in your own browser. PigeonPost keeps only a refresh token in your OS keychain, never your password.

Can opening a message track me?

No. HTML renders in a sandboxed frame that runs no scripts and makes no remote requests. Remote images stay blocked until you load them, so opening a message cannot tell the sender you read it.

It is one developer. What if it stops?

It is GPL-3.0 with the full source on GitHub, free to read, fork or continue. Your mail stays on your IMAP server and your calendar and contacts live in standard files, so nothing is trapped.

Does it work offline?

Yes. Cached mail reads with no connection. Anything you write offline queues in a per-account outbox and delivers when you reconnect.

Only Windows for now?

Windows ships today. macOS and Linux builds are planned.

Will it choke on my huge folders?

No. The message list is virtualised and stays fluid in folders of tens of thousands of messages.

Search that is instant and offline

Search is where the big clients are most disliked. Because your mail is already cached on your machine, PigeonPost searches a local index and answers the moment you type, with no server round-trip.

🔎

Find it now

Search by subject, sender and preview text, ranked by relevance. The index lives on your machine, so results come back instantly and work with no connection.

🔐

Private by the way it is built

Passwords live in your OS keychain, never in a database. Microsoft sign-in keeps only a refresh token there. No PigeonPost cloud sits in the path; your mail is on your server and on your machine.

What is included

The everyday workflows that carry daily use, grouped so you can see the shape at a glance.

📥 Mail

  • Multiple accounts, each a separate inbox, with send-as addresses
  • Rich-text compose with To, Cc and Bcc, per-account signatures, reply, reply-all and forward
  • Attach files or forward a message as an attachment

  • Colour tags that sync across devices as IMAP keywords, plus read, star and junk marks

  • Month, week and day views
  • Recurring events with per-event time zones
  • On-screen reminders and nine emoji categories
  • .ics round-trip with Outlook and Thunderbird
  • Meeting invites over iTIP and iMIP with accept, tentative and decline
  • Teams, Meet, Zoom and Webex join links open in your browser

📇 Contacts

  • A local address book with postal addresses and birthdays
  • vCard and CSV import and export
  • Round-trips with Outlook and Thunderbird

Why I built it

I wanted a desktop client that was free, dependable and laid out the way I actually work. The mainstream options did not fit. Outlook 365 wanted a subscription. Thunderbird is free and open but community-governed, so a bug I hit waits in someone else's queue and the things I reach for most are buried. PigeonPost is my answer: GPL-3.0 and free, a calm predictable interface where everything is easy to find. Oliver Ernster.

A calm promise

Some things PigeonPost will not do, on purpose. Calm and predictable is the whole point, so these stay off the table.

  • No ads. Not now, not later.
  • No ambient AI reading or reordering your mail. Anything AI is opt-in and on demand, never silent.
  • No engagement mechanics. No feeds, no nudges, no streaks.
  • No hosted PigeonPost account or relay. The absence of a cloud in between is the point, not a gap to fill.

Who it is for and who it is not

Honest fit, so the right person trials it and the wrong person is not misled.

For you if

  • You use IMAP or POP3, whether that is self-hosted, your ISP, Fastmail or a corporate server
  • You use Gmail, iCloud, Yahoo, Zoho, Fastmail or StartMail and can add an app password
  • You use a personal Microsoft account (Outlook.com, Hotmail, Live or Microsoft 365) through Microsoft sign-in

Not for you if

  • You only ever want webmail with no desktop install
  • You have a Google Workspace work or school account, which is OAuth-only so an app password will not work
